Support & Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers on Wi-Fi setup, firewall permissions, and legacy devices.

1. Why won't the QR code load on my phone when I scan it?

Both your phone and your Mac must be connected to the exact same Wi-Fi or Ethernet network.

  • Check that your phone hasn't dropped Wi-Fi and switched to cellular data (4G/5G).
  • If your Mac runs a third-party firewall (like Little Snitch or LuLu), make sure it allows incoming local connections on port 8080.
  • Some public or "Guest" Wi-Fi networks block devices from talking to each other directly (client isolation). If so, use your main home/office network or a personal mobile hotspot.

2. Does PopDrop need internet access?

No. Everything happens over your local router. You could unplug your modem from the wall, and as long as your Wi-Fi router is powered on and connecting your local devices, PopDrop will transfer files at full network speed.

3. Where are my files saved?

By default, PopDrop creates a folder inside your Mac's Downloads directory: ~/Downloads/PopDrop.

You can change this anytime by clicking the PopDrop menu bar icon → Settings → Change next to Save to.

4. What if I'm trying to send files from an old device?

If you're using an older device (like a classic phone, e-reader, or basic terminal) where modern JavaScript doesn't load cleanly, PopDrop automatically falls back to /legacy. This is a plain-HTML form that works on almost any browser ever made. You can also visit http://YOUR_MAC_IP:8080/legacy manually anytime.

Need more help or found a bug?

If your issue isn't resolved by the FAQ above or you'd like to request a feature, open an issue directly on GitHub at kiwiprojekt.github.io/issues.
